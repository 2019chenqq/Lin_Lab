Research Direction 01

Treatment-resistant depression and emerging therapeutic strategies

Depression is a major psychiatric disorder that imposes substantial social and economic burdens. Although first-line antidepressants are clinically available, a significant proportion of patients show limited response. This treatment-resistant condition remains a major challenge in clinical psychiatry.

🧠

Disease Model

The laboratory has established a trauma-stress-based animal model that is resistant to first-line antidepressants but responsive to low-dose ketamine treatment.

🔬

Neural Mechanisms

The model shows prefrontal cortical dysfunction and amygdala hyperactivity, findings that are highly consistent with clinical observations from collaborating medical teams.

Non-invasive treatment: rTMS, iTBS, and cTBS

The laboratory collaborates with clinical teams to study repetitive transcranial magnetic stimulation, or rTMS, in both patients and animal models. Intermittent theta burst stimulation, or iTBS, has shown promising therapeutic effects and has been approved for clinical use in Taiwan.

In animal models of treatment-resistant depression, iTBS improves abnormalities in synaptic plasticity and may act through the regulation of brain-derived neurotrophic factor. Continuous theta burst stimulation, or cTBS, also improves depressive-like behaviors, but its mechanism appears to be more closely associated with inhibitory synaptic plasticity and proBDNF regulation.

Future Direction

The laboratory will further examine newly synthesized compounds from a pharmacological perspective and identify key neurotransmitter systems involved in treatment-resistant depression. The long-term goal is to develop effective treatment strategies and clinical predictors of therapeutic response.

Research Direction 02

Autism spectrum disorder and neurodevelopmental disease

Autism spectrum disorder is a neurodevelopmental condition characterized by social deficits, communication impairment, and restricted or repetitive behaviors. Imbalance between excitatory and inhibitory neurotransmission, as well as synaptic functional or structural deficits, is considered central to the disease phenotype.

🐁

Autism-like Animal Models

The laboratory uses rodent models with autism-like behaviors to investigate pathological mechanisms underlying brain dysfunction and to identify potential therapeutic approaches.

⚡

Neural Transmission Balance

The team studies glutamatergic signaling and NMDA receptor-mediated excitatory synaptic plasticity from pharmacological and neurobiological perspectives.

NMDA receptors, electrical stimulation, and synaptic regulation

The laboratory has shown that either partial blockade of NMDA receptors or indirect modulation of downstream molecular pathways can improve autism-like behavioral phenotypes.

The lab also investigates deep brain stimulation, which generates micro-scale electrical circuits in the brain and modulates neural transmission balance, thereby improving autism-like behavioral symptoms in animal models.

Environmental factors and transgenerational effects

Recent studies examine how environmental exposure during early brain development may affect autism-related phenotypes. Findings suggest that social deficits and abnormal excitatory-inhibitory synaptic regulation in first-generation autism-like mice may extend to the second generation, highlighting the importance of inhibitory synaptic homeostasis in maintaining social function across generations.

Future Direction

Future studies will further investigate core pathological mechanisms during development and identify key targets for therapeutic intervention in autism spectrum disorder and related neurodevelopmental conditions.

Methods & Translational Medicine

From animal behavior to neural circuits, building a bridge toward clinical translation.

The laboratory establishes animal models of psychiatric and neurodevelopmental disorders and collaborates with clinical medicine to identify novel therapeutic targets and disease mechanisms.

🐁

Animal Behavior

Behavioral assays are used to analyze depression-, autism-, and neurodevelopment-related phenotypes and connect them with neural activity.

📡

Electrophysiology

Electrophysiological approaches are used to investigate neural excitability, inhibitory regulation, and abnormal synaptic plasticity.

🧬

Emerging Platforms

The lab aims to integrate calcium imaging, chemogenetics, and optogenetics to examine disease-related behaviors at the circuit level.

Translational Goal

By integrating animal models, neuroscience techniques, and clinical collaboration, the laboratory aims to develop novel pharmacological and therapeutic strategies for psychiatric and neurodevelopmental disorders.
