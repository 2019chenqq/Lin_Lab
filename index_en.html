About

Connecting fundamental neuroscience, translational medicine, and scientific training.

Prof. Lin’s laboratory focuses on psychobiomedicine, neuroscience, and pharmacology. By combining disease animal models with neuroscience approaches, the lab investigates mechanisms underlying psychiatric and neurodevelopmental disorders.

🔬

Research Philosophy

The laboratory centers on animal behavior, electrophysiology, and neural signaling. Starting from disease-related behavioral phenotypes, the lab investigates how neural activity, synaptic plasticity, and molecular regulation shape physiological and behavioral responses.

Through collaboration with clinical teams, the laboratory aims to establish translational research models and identify novel therapeutic targets and treatment strategies for psychiatric and neurodevelopmental disorders.

Research

Linking disease animal models with psychobiomedicine, neuroscience, and clinical translation.

The laboratory focuses on psychiatric and neurodevelopmental disorders, integrating animal behavior, electrophysiology, neural signaling, and pharmacology to investigate disease mechanisms and emerging therapeutic strategies.

🐁

Disease Animal Models

The laboratory uses animal models of psychiatric and neurodevelopmental disorders to analyze disease-related behavioral phenotypes and connect them with neural activity, synaptic plasticity, and pathological mechanisms.

Animal Behavior Disease Models
🧠

Treatment-Resistant Depression

The lab investigates animal models resistant to first-line antidepressants, focusing on prefrontal cortical deficits, amygdala hyperactivity, synaptic plasticity abnormalities, and emerging treatment mechanisms.

Depression Translational Medicine
⚡

Autism and Neurodevelopment

Using rodent models with autism-like behaviors, the lab investigates neural transmission imbalance, synaptic dysfunction, and transgenerational effects to identify potential therapeutic strategies.
